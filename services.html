Medical Services

General Medicine

General medicine refers to the branch of medicine that focuses on the overall healthcare of individuals, providing comprehensive and primary medical care. General practitioners or internists in this field diagnose and treat a wide range of common illnesses and conditions, offering preventive care, managing chronic diseases, and referring patients to specialists when necessary.

Specialized Care

Specialized care refers to medical services provided by healthcare professionals who have advanced training and expertise in a specific area of medicine. These specialists diagnose, treat, and manage complex or uncommon medical conditions, providing specialized knowledge and skills tailored to the unique needs of patients.

Diagnostic Services

Diagnostic services involve the use of medical tests, procedures, and imaging technologies to identify and diagnose diseases, conditions, or injuries. These services may include laboratory testing, radiology examinations, pathology analysis, and other diagnostic procedures, helping healthcare providers make informed decisions regarding treatment and care plans.

Preventive Medicine

Preventive medicine focuses on proactive measures to prevent the occurrence or progression of diseases and promote overall health and well-being. It encompasses various strategies such as vaccinations, screenings, health education, lifestyle modifications, and risk factor management, aiming to reduce the incidence of illness and improve population health outcomes.

Rehabilitation and Physical Therapy

Rehabilitation and physical therapy involve therapeutic interventions to restore or enhance physical functioning and mobility in individuals with disabilities, injuries, or medical conditions. These services are provided by skilled professionals who utilize exercises, manual techniques, assistive devices, and other modalities to improve strength, flexibility, coordination, and overall quality of life.

Dental Care

Dental care refers to the specialized medical care focused on oral health. Dental professionals, such as dentists and dental hygienists, diagnose and treat conditions related to teeth, gums, and other oral tissues. Services provided may include routine check-ups, cleanings, fillings, extractions, orthodontics, and various dental procedures to maintain oral hygiene and address oral health issues.

Wellness Programs

Wellness programs are comprehensive initiatives designed to promote and enhance overall well-being, encompassing physical, mental, and emotional health. These programs often include health assessments, lifestyle counseling, stress management techniques, nutrition guidance, fitness activities, and other wellness-oriented interventions to help individuals achieve optimal health and prevent illness.

Emergency Services

Emergency services are medical services provided in urgent or life-threatening situations. These services are available 24/7 and involve rapid assessment, stabilization, and treatment of critical conditions. Emergency departments and paramedics play a vital role in providing immediate care for severe injuries, sudden illnesses, acute exacerbations of chronic conditions, and other emergencies.

Drug Supply

Drug supply refers to the provision of medications and pharmaceutical products to patients, ensuring their access to essential drugs for treatment, symptom management, and disease prevention. This includes the procurement, storage, distribution, and dispensing of medications in accordance with regulatory standards and patient needs.

Day Hospital

A day hospital, also known as a day surgery center or ambulatory care center, offers medical procedures and treatments that do not require overnight hospitalization. Patients receive specialized care and undergo diagnostic tests, surgeries, infusions, or other medical interventions, returning home on the same day. Day hospitals provide a convenient and cost-effective alternative to inpatient care for selected medical conditions.

Health Insurance

Health insurance is a financial product that provides coverage for medical expenses, offering individuals or groups protection against high healthcare costs. Health insurance policies typically cover a range of services, including preventive care, hospitalization, specialist consultations, medications, and other healthcare needs. It helps individuals manage healthcare expenses and ensures access to necessary medical services.

Remote Consulting

Remote consulting, also known as telemedicine or telehealth, enables patients to consult with healthcare providers remotely using technology and communication tools. Through virtual appointments or online platforms, patients can receive medical advice, diagnosis and treatment recommendations
